Learn the laws of lashon hara (evil speech) from the Chofetz Chaim. -Submitted by Moshe. Our sages compare harmful speech to the most serious sins — and teach that habitual gossip can destroy relationships, communities, and a person's own spiritual standing. The Chofetz Chaim devoted his life to showing how the Torah warns about speech again and again, because words can build worlds or tear them down. Even one day of guarding your tongue can transform how you see other people — and how they see you.

Eat some chametz (leavened food) and have in mind that you're fulfilling the mitzvah of cleaning your home of chametz for Pesach. If it is currently Pesach, eat some matzah! On the night before Pesach, Jewish families search every corner of the home by candlelight for any last crumb of chametz — because no leaven may remain in the house during the seven days of the holiday. We left Egypt in such a rush that our bread had no time to rise, and clearing out chametz each year reconnects us to that moment of freedom. What you're chewing today is part of that ancient story, if you have in mind you're doing it in honor of the holiday.

Give the next Jewish person you see a blessing. If you can't think of anything, just bless them that they should have a long and healthy life! In the Temple, the Kohanim would raise their hands and bless the entire nation with words of peace and protection that we still hear in synagogue today. You don't have to be a Kohen to wish someone well — a heartfelt blessing from an ordinary Jew is a beautiful act of love. When you bless another person, you become a channel for good in the world.

Actively oppose idolatry! 🔨 The Rambam explains in Chapter 7 that we have a positive commandment to destroy items of idol worship and everything used for it. Here's something powerful: We don't just avoid idol worship - we actively work to eliminate it! Did you know? Even the wood and stones from an idol must be destroyed. Today's mission: Take an active stand against negative influences in your life.
