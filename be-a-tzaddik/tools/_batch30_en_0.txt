Get ready to experience the Sukkah spirit! ⛺️ During Sukkot, we have the special mitzvah of dwelling in a Sukkah – a temporary booth – just like camping, but with a huge spiritual punch! This unique dwelling reminds us of the clouds of glory that protected us in the desert and the fragility of life, encouraging us to trust completely in Hashem. What does 'dwelling' mean? It primarily means eating your meals there. For sleeping, the Shulchan Aruch (Orach Chaim 639) rules that men are obligated to sleep in the Sukkah, but women are exempt (though many choose to sleep there if comfortable). Sephardi practice is generally to be more stringent about sleeping in the Sukkah even if uncomfortable, while Ashkenazim are more lenient if there's significant discomfort (e.g., cold, rain, or a bad smell). The Talmud teaches that the Shechinah (Divine Presence) literally rests in a kosher Sukkah – no wonder it's a favorite holiday for so many! So, even if you can't build a full Sukkah, reflect on its themes of impermanence and Divine protection.
