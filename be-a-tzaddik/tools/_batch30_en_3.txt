Learn about the Torah's obligations of respect for Torah scholars and the elderly — with specific, practical rules! 🙌 'You shall rise before the aged and honor the face of the elder' (Leviticus 19:32). Two separate mitzvot here: rising for the elderly and rising for Torah scholars — with different rules for each (customs do vary, ask your local rav). For ELDERS (over 70): you must stand, or at least rise up slightly as a token of respect when they approach within 4 cubits (about 2 meters or 6-8 feet). The standing obligation ends when they pass. For TORAH SCHOLARS (talmid chacham): you must stand (if you're physically able) even if they're younger than 70, as long as they have significant Torah knowledge. For a Gadol (one of the leading rabbis of the generation) you should stand up as soon as you see them enter the room, and not sit until they reach their place. It's also important not to make fun of or embarrass a Torah scholar. The Talmud says someone who does this does not have a portion in Olam Haba (the World to Come)! Your mission: next time you see an elderly Jewish person, practice showing them a little more respect. It's a mitzvah!
