Learn about the Torah obligation to TEACH — not just to learn — and the specific mitzvah of 'v'shinantam l'vanecha'! 📚 The Torah says 'And you shall teach them carefully to your children' (Deuteronomy 6:7). This is a positive commandment on every Jewish adult to teach Torah — and specifically, the obligation is first to your own children. The Rambam rules (Hilchot Talmud Torah 1:1): a father is obligated to teach his son Torah. If the father cannot teach, he must hire a teacher. When does the obligation begin? As soon as the child can speak! The first words you teach a child should be Torah — traditionally the Torah verse 'Torah tzivah lanu Moshe' and Shema. Extended meaning: the Talmud rules 'l'vanecha' means both biological children AND students. Teaching students is like raising spiritual children. The mitzvah also applies to each person themselves: you must learn in order to be able to teach. One fascinating ruling: you are NOT permitted to charge money for teaching Torah itself. A teacher may charge for their time away from other work (as opportunity cost), but direct payment FOR Torah teaching is frowned upon and considered a form of desecrating the Torah. This is why many Talmud teachers historically were supported by community funds rather than student tuition.
