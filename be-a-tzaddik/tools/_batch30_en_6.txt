Learn about the world's first mandatory RSVP event - the Korban Pesach! 🐑 This wasn’t just a mitzvah, it was a logistical miracle. Imagine hundreds of thousands of people descending on Jerusalem all with one goal: barbecuing enough meat to feed the entire nation! 🌃 But you couldn't just stroll in and buy a couple lamb chops. You had to be registered on a specific group's animal before the sun even went down. Every single person had to be part of a 'chaburah' or registered group. If you weren’t on the list you weren’t eating! 📝 On the afternoon of the 14th of Nissan pretty much every able-bodied Jew in the country showed up at the Temple at once. To handle this holy rush hour, the priests split everyone into three massive shifts. As soon as the first group packed the courtyard the heavy doors swung shut and the service began!  This is where it gets cinematic because the Kohanim (priests) stood in perfectly orchestrated lines reaching all the way to the altar. One row held only gold bowls and the next only silver. As lambs or goats were offered, the blood was passed hand to hand down the line like a relay race! Once your animal was slaughtered and its blood sprinkled on the altar, you got your animal back. Then each group went to where they were staying, and needed to roast their meat whole, entirely over an open fire and hold a Pesach seder! And forget the carving knife because you were strictly forbidden from breaking a single bone! It had to stay whole and regal. 🦴 The pressure was on to finish by the midnight deadline while the whole city erupted in the singing of Hallel. Jerusalem was so packed the rooftops were covered in people and the smell of roasted lamb filled the air! It was a massive roaring celebration of freedom! May we merit to experience it ourselves speedily in our days as soon as the Temple is rebuilt! Learn more! 🎊🍷
