Guard your body, mind, and soul — they're gifts from Hashem! 💫 This mitzvah isn't just about physical health; it's a holistic approach to serving G-d. The Rambam (Hilchot De'ot 3:3) teaches that maintaining your health is a Torah obligation, because 'it is impossible to understand and know G-d when one is sick or hungry.' Your body is the vessel for your soul's mission — a fit body supports a healthy spiritual life. For your MIND: engage in Torah study, which sharpens your intellect and purifies your thoughts. Avoid excessive worry and grudges, as they can harm both mind and body. For your SOUL: nurture it with regular prayer, mitzvot, and acts of kindness, strengthening its connection to its Divine Source. Simcha (joy) is also a crucial spiritual obligation; sadness can be a major barrier to spiritual growth. Practical takeaway: eat well, get enough sleep, exercise, and constantly seek joy. These aren't luxuries; they're spiritual necessities! Source: Deuteronomy 4:15; Rambam, Hilchot De'ot 3:3; Sefer HaChinuch, Mitzvah 225.
