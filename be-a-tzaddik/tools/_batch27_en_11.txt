Explore #3 of the Constant Mitzvot: Know that Hashem is One! ☝️ This isn't just about a numerical unity; it's about understanding that God is absolutely unique, indivisible, and utterly beyond our comprehension – the ultimate singularity! He is the source of all existence, which is completely unified in His essence and power. When you say the first line of Shema Yisrael, focus deeply on this profound truth. Many have the custom to close their eyes during this line to block out distractions and concentrate entirely on Hashem's absolute unity. You can fulfill this mitzvah any time by contemplating God's oneness and His singular control over the universe. Source: Deuteronomy 6:4; Rambam, Sefer HaMitzvot, Positive Commandment 2; Mishneh Torah, Yesodei HaTorah 1:7.
