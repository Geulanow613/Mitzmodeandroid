Learn about the divine command to have the best time ever! 🎊 The Torah doesn't just suggest you have fun on festival days—it literally commands it with the mitzvah of Simchat Yom Tov! This applies to the "Big Three" festivals: Pesach, Shavuot, and Sukkot! 🗓️ Since "there is no real joy without meat and wine," the holiday menu is basically a holy duty. 🍷🥩 But the halacha knows that everyone finds joy differently so it breaks down the party plan for the whole family! For men the focus is on delicious meat and wine. For women the mitzvah is fulfilled through getting new clothes or jewelry specifically for the holiday! For the kids it’s all about the sugar rush—giving them treats and nuts to keep the festive vibes high! 🍬 The man of the house is required to provide these things for his family according to his means! Our sages also warn that if you’re just feasting with your own family and locking the door you’re not doing "holiday joy" but just "stomach joy." To do it right you’ve got to pull up an extra chair for the poor and the lonely because true happiness is only real when it’s shared! At the very least you should give tzedaka to a Jewish person in need so they can enjoy the festival too. Learn more and get ready for the next festival, whenever that may be!
