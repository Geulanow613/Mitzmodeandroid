Ready to launch into Shabbat with holiness? 🍷 Learn about Kiddush, the beautiful mitzvah that sanctifies our holy day! This isn't just a ritual; it's a Torah-level commandment to 'remember the Shabbat day to sanctify it' (Exodus 20:8). The Sages beautifully established this with Kiddush over a cup of wine. Here's how to rock your Kiddush, even if you're out of wine: (1) Kiddush B'Makom Seudah: You *must* recite Kiddush exactly where you'll be eating your meal! Making Kiddush in the living room and then sprinting to the kitchen for challah can be a real halachic oopsie! (2) Minimum Drink: You need to drink at least a 'melo lugmav' (approximately a cheekful or 2 fl oz.) of the Kiddush beverage. (3) Women's Obligation: Ladies, you're equally obligated! Even though it's a time-bound positive commandment (which often exempts women), women were part of the Exodus miracle that Shabbat commemorates. (4) Grape Juice: Yes, grape juice is perfectly valid for Kiddush and fully satisfies the obligation for everyone! (5) Kiddush Alternatives: The rules for alternatives differ between Friday night and Shabbat day. On Friday night, if wine/grape juice isn't available, **challah (bread)** is the next best option (make sure to wash hands first and say 'HaMotzi' in place of 'Borei Pri HaGafen'). On Shabbat morning, however, challah *cannot* be used as a substitute for Kiddush; instead, an 'important beverage' (chamor medina) like beer, whiskey, or even coffee/tea in certain communities (check with your Rav!) can be used!
