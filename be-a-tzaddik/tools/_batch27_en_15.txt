Learn how to light Shabbat candles! 🕯️ The mitzvah of lighting Shabbat candles ushers in the holy day, bringing peace, warmth, and a special spiritual light into your home. This beautiful act, performed by Jewish women (or men if no woman is available) before sunset on Friday, marks the transition from the mundane week to the sacred Shabbat. The Torah describes Shabbat as a sign between Hashem and Am Yisrael, a perpetual reminder of Creation and our unique covenant. The halacha (Jewish law) dictates a fascinating custom: after lighting the candles, we wave our hands and cover our eyes. This allows us to recite the blessing over the candles *before* we visually benefit from their light. Normally, a blessing precedes the mitzvah, but reciting the blessing itself ushers in Shabbat, after which lighting candles would be forbidden! So, we light, cover our eyes (effectively delaying our acceptance of Shabbat's holiness until after the blessing), say the blessing, and then uncover our eyes to experience the mitzvah fully. It's a brilliant halachic workaround! Now you know why we do it!
