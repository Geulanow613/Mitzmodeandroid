Learn about shechitah — kosher slaughter — and the five requirements that must all be met or the meat is forbidden! 🔪 The five invalidating factors of shechitah, remembered by the Hebrew acronym ShaCHaT NaDaH: (1) Shehiyah — pausing mid-cut. The cut must be one continuous smooth motion. Stop in the middle, even briefly, and it's potentially treif. (2) Chaladah — 'burrowing.' The knife cannot go underneath the trachea into the throat; it must cut cleanly across the surface, and the knife must be completely visible. It cannot be inserted under a flap of skin or between the trachea and esophagus (3) Drasha — pressing too hard. The knife must GLIDE, not be forced. If the shochet pushes rather than draws the blade, it's invalid. (4) Hagramah — cutting in the wrong location. There's a very precise section of the throat where the cut is valid — too high or too low and it's invalid. (5) Ikur — tearing. The windpipe or esophagus cannot be ripped out before the cut is complete. And the knife itself? It must be razor-sharp with a perfectly smooth edge — no nicks. The knife is so sharp it can put a high-quality chef’s knife or surgical scalpel to shame! Those often have a tiny, saw-like jaggedness. That microscopic 'saw' is actually what helps a kitchen knife bite into a tomato skin. For a chalaf, that jaggedness is strictly forbidden. The blade must be polished to a mirror-like, frictionless finish. If there is even a miniscule burr, nick, or unevenness on the edge, the knife will tear the animal's tissue rather than slice it cleanly, instantly rendering the meat non-kosher. Kosher meat is a high 'steaks' biz! 😂
